What each stage is worth

Feature-to-outcome, stage by stage — the pain it kills, with the actual tools and hours named, straight from the April–May interviews.

Sale — one pricing engine, AI takeoff
What it doesEvery quote comes from one engine; the AI takeoff & estimating skill feeds clean numbers in; colors and scope lock before the job moves.
Pain it eliminates todayThree estimating methods in one company — the Eighth Main worksheet, Ogden pricing "all in their heads," Elko's copied master sheet — then everything retyped into QuickBooks line by line, and contracts retyped by hand from the QB estimate.
Quantified impact (est.)Stops custom-build mispricing — "miss something on a $400,000 building and that could be all of your profit." Ends months-late discoveries like a 7% markup on insulation surfacing at a 6-month closeout. Takeoffs stop being a one-person bottleneck.
Who it servesBrett, Thad, Brad — and Accounting downstream.
Preconstruction — reads the record, never rebuilds it
What it doesNotified automatically when the deal closes; permits, colors, and materials tracked on the same record — nothing retyped.
Pain it eliminates todayInheriting what the sale didn't finish; a weekly phone-chase of every active client because email lands in spam; permit corrections triaged from scratch by one person; the same job retyped across 8+ checklists, trackers, and the job list.
Quantified impact (est.)The retype chain across checklist → job list → trackers dies; permit corrections start from the record, not from zero; the single-point-of-failure risk on the job list and the jurisdiction knowledge drops sharply.
Who it servesKandis, Alexa, Courtney, Dawn.
Build — assigned automatically, status from the field
What it doesPM assigned the moment a job is ready; status is a live field on a phone; pay sheets and crew schedules built from the record, not re-read off the plans.
Pain it eliminates todayVerbal Monday-meeting ownership — miss the row and a job has no owner; a PM on the road 60–65% of the week opening "three different spots to just get an overview of my job"; weekly crew payroll consuming 1–1.5 days of one person, every week.
Quantified impact (est.)Payroll from ~1.5 days/week toward hours; zero ownerless jobs; one-tap job overview from the truck instead of multi-tab Excel on a phone.
Who it servesBronson, Kacey, the foremen.
Accounting — synced, not retyped
What it doesQuickBooks connected; payment received starts closeout automatically; the job-cost / CFO skill pulls P&L and "how did the last two weeks look" on demand.
Pain it eliminates todayThe same number keyed into "six different" of ~900 spreadsheets; reports pulled from several systems, hand-typed, then re-run to catch the inevitable error; the controller doing real number work after hours; 25–30 print-handwrite-retype closeouts a month.
Quantified impact (est.)Double entry stops. Closeouts start themselves. The after-hours reporting load collapses, and coding errors get caught by the system instead of a third manual pass.
Who it servesMindy, Cade — and the numbers Chad reads.
Close — margin in real time
What it doesMargin lands as the job closes and feeds straight back into the next quote.
Pain it eliminates todayCloseouts lagging 30+ days by design; leadership reviewing — and paying commissions on — numbers ~45 days stale; closeout lessons never reaching bidding: "your last six months worth of quoting everything is going to be wrong."
Quantified impact (est.)Pricing corrections in days instead of quarters; commissions paid on real numbers; the margin leak visible while it can still be fixed.
Who it servesChad, Brett, every estimator.
